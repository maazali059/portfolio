About Enernomics

Purpose, scope, and roadmap.

Purpose

Enernomics is a decision-support framework that replicates the workflow an energy and infrastructure consultant follows — from project screening and technical assumptions, through financial modeling and risk assessment, to an investment recommendation with stated reasoning and confidence, not just a number.

Version 1.2 Scope

Two domains are built deep rather than broad: EV charging infrastructure investment, and renewable energy procurement under India's Green Energy Open Access framework (Captive / Group Captive / Third-Party comparison). Around both, this version adds a project screening questionnaire (feeding directly into risk scores), an option comparison view for the EV domain, one-at-a-time tornado sensitivity analysis, per-input source citations, and PDF executive report export. The architecture was designed specifically so a second domain and these cross-cutting features could be added without restructuring the platform.

Deliberately Not Built — and Why

These were considered and explicitly left out, not overlooked:

  • Monte Carlo / probabilistic simulation. Doing this honestly requires choosing and defending probability distributions and correlation structure between risk factors — a materially different (and much deeper) analytical claim than the deterministic scenario analysis this platform actually does. Rather than add a shallow, hard-to-defend version, V1.2 keeps Base/Optimistic/Pessimistic scenarios and the tornado chart as the tools for reasoning about uncertainty.
  • GIS / location suitability scoring. Doing this honestly requires real spatial data and defensible siting criteria and weights — neither of which this platform has. Site selection stays a qualitative input (via Project Screening) rather than a manufactured spatial score.

Roadmap (Not Built Yet)

  • Additional domains: rooftop/utility solar sizing, battery storage sizing
  • Downloadable Excel assumptions workbook
  • Editable risk category weights (currently equal-weighted)
  • Full state-by-state CSS and banking-rule database (currently 4 states + generic fallback)
This platform was developed as an independent analytical framework to demonstrate structured decision-support methodologies commonly used in energy and infrastructure consulting. It is intended for educational and portfolio purposes and does not represent any commercial consulting product or real company.
